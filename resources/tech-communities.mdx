Online communities are a great way to learn, get support, and build connections — especially when you are starting out. These are all free to join.

## NZ Tech Slack & Discord Communities

These are where NZ tech professionals communicate daily. Jobs are often shared here before they appear on job boards.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="DevelopNZ Slack" icon="slack" href="https://dev.elop.nz">
    The largest Slack workspace for NZ developers. The **#jobs** channel regularly has roles posted before they appear on Seek. Also has channels for JavaScript, React, Rust, software design, and more.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Tech Community Slacks (GitHub)" icon="github" href="https://github.com/thisdot/tech-community-slacks">
    A curated directory of tech Slack communities worldwide. Find groups for your specific language, framework, or interest area.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
When joining a new Slack or Discord community, introduce yourself in the #introductions channel. Mention what you are learning and what you are interested in — the NZ tech community is very welcoming to newcomers.
</Tip>

## LinkedIn Groups

LinkedIn has many active tech groups where you can learn from others, share ideas, and discover job opportunities. Search for groups like:

- **"NZ Tech"** or **"New Zealand Technology"** — General NZ tech discussions
- **"Women in Tech NZ"** — Support and networking for women in the NZ tech industry
- **"Auckland IT Professionals"** — Local networking and job sharing
- **"NZ Startups"** — Startup ecosystem news and opportunities

To find and join groups:

1. Go to [linkedin.com/groups](https://www.linkedin.com/groups)
2. Search for topics that interest you
3. Join groups that are active and welcoming to beginners
4. Engage with posts and introduce yourself

## Exercise 1: Write a Cover Letter with AI

**Goal**: Use an AI writing assistant to draft a tailored cover letter.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a job listing">
    Go to [Seek](https://www.seek.co.nz) or [Indeed](https://nz.indeed.com) and find a job listing that interests you. Copy the job description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open an AI assistant">
    Go to [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai). Create a free account if you do not have one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your prompt">
    Paste the job description and ask the AI to help you write a cover letter. For example: *"Help me write a cover letter for this role. I have experience in [your experience]. Keep it professional and under one page."*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and personalise">
    Read through the AI's draft carefully. Edit it to sound like you — add your own experiences, adjust the tone, and correct any details.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Exercise 2: AI-Powered Job Search

**Goal**: Use AI features on job platforms to find relevant roles.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set up job alerts">
    On Seek or Indeed, set up AI-powered job alerts based on your skills and preferences.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optimise your profile">
    Use LinkedIn's AI suggestions to improve your profile headline and summary.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explore recommendations">
    Review the AI-recommended jobs on each platform. Note which ones match your goals and why.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Exercise 3: Explore an AI Tool

**Goal**: Get comfortable using an AI assistant for everyday tasks.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a tool">
    Pick one of the AI tools introduced in the workshop (ChatGPT, Claude, or Grammarly).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask it questions">
    Try asking the tool to explain a concept, summarise an article, or help you practise interview answers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reflect">
    Think about how this tool could help you in your job search. What worked well? What surprised you?
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Remember: AI is a tool to support you, not to do the work for you. Always review, personalise, and fact-check any AI-generated content.
</Note>
